    Section 1.2 Full-time Equivalence and College Funding

    The previous section established the definitions and calcualtions for full-time equivalence. In this section, the impact that FTE has on college-level funding and how funds are allocated based on FTE is discussed. When reading this informaiton, it is important to keep in mind that the information and numbers represent the information available at the time of writing. Legislation may impact the values of the calculations between times when this document is updated.

    Subsection 1.2.1 Tiered Funding Based on FTE

    Each course in the North Carolina Community College System has various and expectations for time needed for instruction are accounted for in the Contact Hours of the course.

    Fact 1.2.1. Tier Funding for FTE.

    Certain courses in the NCCCS are associated with higher costs. This higher cost is adjusted in the college’s allocated funds based on a tiered system of funding. The NCCCS uses a four-tier system. The following are the four tiers with their respective funding level Click here to see the Fiscal Research Division of the NC General Assembly presentation on these numbers. 1 .
    • Tier 1A ($4,889.65): This is the highest level of funding tier. Courses in this tier include health care and technical education courses.
    • Tier 1B ($4,325.46): Courses in this tier include lab-based science courses, college-level mathematics courses, and some courses in health care and technical education not funded at the Tier 1A level.
    • Tier 2 ($3,721.27): This tier includes all other curriculum courses, basic skills, and other continuing education courses scheduled for more than 96 or more contact hours leading to a credential, certification or indurstry-designed curriculum.
    • Tier 3 ($2,380.88): This tier is for all other continuing education courses.

    Subsection 1.2.2 Calculating Budget FTE

    The budget allocations based on FTE is calculated by multiplying the FTE of a course by the funding alloted for that course’s tier. As an equation, this looks like
    \begin{equation*} \textbf{FTE Budget Allocation} = \textbf{FTE} \times \textbf{Tier Funding Allocation} \end{equation*}
    Using the tiers for courses, the amount of an institution’s budget that is allocated based on the FTE can be calculated. Using the information from Example 1.1.9, the budget allocation for the course can be calculated. The following example illustrates this calculation.

    Example 1.2.2. Budget Allocation for ENG-111 with 21 Students.

    ENG-111 according to the NCCCCS is classified as a tier two course for FTE budget allocations. In the previous example with ENG-111 and the number of students equal to 21, the FTE was calculated to equal 1.969. Therefore, based on the tiered funding this particular section of ENG-111 would receive
    The total funding allocation based on the teired funding and the FTE of an ENG-111 course with the number of students equal to 21 would receive a budget allocation of
    \begin{align*} \textbf{Budget Allocation} \amp = \textbf{FTE} \times \textbf{Tier Funding Allocation} \\ \amp = 1.969 \times 3721.27 \\ \amp = 7327.18 \end{align*}
    Therefore, when determing a college’s funding allocation from the NCCCS State Office, this particular section of ENG-111 would generate $7,327.18 dollars in budget allocations.

    Subsection 1.2.3 Calculating Return on Investment (ROI)

    Another metric by which institutions and organizagion measure the financial outcomes of the services they offer is called the Return on Investment (ROI). For consistency, consider the following as the definition of return on investment for these notes.

    Definition 1.2.3. Return on Investment (ROI).

    Return on Investment is a performance metric used to calculate and compare a the financial efficiency and the net budgetary profits of a course. The calculation for ROI is as follows:
    \begin{equation*} \textbf{ROI} = \frac{\textbf{FTE Budget Allocation} - \textbf{Instructional Cost}}{\textbf{FTE Budget Allocation}}\times 100\% \end{equation*}
    For simplicity, the ROI of a course can taken to be the numerator of the ROI calculation, rather than the traditional percentage. However, if the purpose of the ROI metric is to compare two courses, then the percentage should be used to rather than simply the difference in the allocation and cost.

    Example 1.2.4. Calculating ROI of ENG-111 with 21 Students.

    Once again, return to Example 1.1.9 and Example 1.2.2. In those examples, it was calculated that the ENG-111 course with 21 students would receive a budget allocation of $7,327.18. Suppose that the cose to run the course, including instructional contract, FICA, and supply costs total to $1,948.18. Then the ROI of the course would be
    \begin{align*} \textbf{ROI} \amp = \textbf{FTE Budget Allocation} - \textbf{Instructional Cost} \\ \amp = 7327.18-1948.18 \\ \amp = 5379.00 \end{align*}
    In the traditional sense of ROI, it would be concluded that the course has a ROI of about 73.4%.
