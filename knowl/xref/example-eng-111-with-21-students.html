Example 1.1.5.

Consider an ENG-111: Writing and Inquiry course that originally had 24 students on the roster at the start date of the course. Now consider, as you may have witnessed, that two students drop the course before the Census Date, and another student never participated in any part of the course by the Census Date. This means that 21 students are participating in the course at the Census Date; therefore, the Number of Students in the course would be 21.
For ENG-111, the number of contact hours is set at three hours. Therefore, the Student Membership Hours (CMH) for ENG-111, is calclated to be
\begin{equation*} \textbf{ENG-111's SMH} = 3 \times 16 = 48. \end{equation*}
Furthermore, the course membership hours would be
\begin{equation*} \textbf{ENG-111 with 21 Number of Students' CMH} = 48 \times 21 = 1008 \end{equation*}
